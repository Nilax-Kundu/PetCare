Cat Grooming 101

Published on April 10, 2025

Grooming is an essential part of your cat's health and wellbeing. Regular grooming not only keeps their coat clean and shiny but also prevents health issues such as matted fur, skin infections, and hairballs.

Start with brushing. Most cats enjoy being brushed once they get used to it. Short-haired cats may only need brushing once a week, while long-haired breeds may require daily grooming.

Next, check their ears for wax buildup or signs of infection. Use a vet-approved solution if cleaning is needed. Don't forget their nails—trim them every couple of weeks to prevent overgrowth and discomfort.

Bathing isn't usually necessary unless your cat is especially dirty or has a medical condition. If you do bathe them, use a shampoo specifically made for cats and ensure the water is warm—not hot.

Lastly, always observe your cat’s reaction. Grooming should be a bonding experience. If they’re distressed, slow down, and try again later.

Tip: Use treats and positive reinforcement to help your cat associate grooming with good things!
