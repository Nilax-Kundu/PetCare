How often should I feed my dog?

+

Most adult dogs should be fed twice a day, once in the morning and once in the evening. Puppies under 6 months of age may need to be fed 3-4 times a day. The amount of food depends on your dog's size, age, breed, and activity level. Always follow the feeding guidelines on your dog food packaging and consult with your veterinarian for specific recommendations.

How often should I walk my dog?

+

Most dogs need at least one or two walks daily, but this varies by breed, age, and energy level. High-energy breeds like Border Collies or Huskies may need several hours of exercise daily, while lower-energy breeds like Bulldogs may be satisfied with shorter walks. Senior dogs may need shorter, more frequent walks. In addition to physical exercise, dogs also need mental stimulation through play and training.

How often should I clean my cat's litter box?

+

Litter boxes should be scooped at least once daily, and the litter should be completely changed once a week. If you have multiple cats, you may need to clean more frequently. A good rule of thumb is to have one more litter box than the number of cats in your household. Regular cleaning helps prevent odor and encourages your cat to use the litter box consistently.

Do cats need baths?

+

Most cats are excellent self-groomers and don't need regular baths. However, there are exceptions: long-haired breeds may need occasional baths to prevent matting, and cats with certain health conditions or mobility issues may need help staying clean. If your cat gets into something sticky or smelly, a bath might be necessary. Use cat-specific shampoo and make the experience as stress-free as possible.

How often should I take my pet to the vet?

+

Adult dogs and cats should visit the veterinarian at least once a year for a wellness check-up. Puppies and kittens need more frequent visits for vaccinations and health monitoring, typically every 3-4 weeks until they're about 16 weeks old. Senior pets (generally over 7 years for dogs, over 10 for cats) benefit from twice-yearly check-ups. Always take your pet to the vet if you notice any concerning symptoms or changes in behavior.

How can I tell if my pet is sick?

+

Common signs of illness in pets include changes in appetite (eating more or less than usual), increased thirst, lethargy, vomiting, diarrhea, coughing, sneezing, difficulty breathing, changes in urination habits, and unusual behavior. You know your pet best, so any significant change from their normal behavior could indicate a problem. When in doubt, consult with your veterinarian.

What should I do if my pet eats something toxic?

+

If you suspect your pet has ingested something toxic, contact your veterinarian or an animal poison control center immediately. Common household toxins for pets include chocolate, xylitol (found in sugar-free gum and some peanut butters), grapes/raisins, onions, garlic, certain plants, and many medications. Don't wait for symptoms to appear, as prompt treatment is crucial. Try to identify what and how much your pet consumed to help the veterinarian determine the best course of action.
